Chapter 2: Components

CPU: Intel i7 7700 ($350)

For around $350 CAD, this CPU is perfect for the average consumer-turned-editor.

A cheaper alternative would be the Ryzen 7 Processor. If you're looking to overclock, the i7 7700K is also a great choice.

GPU: Zotac GTX 1060 6GB Mini ($150 CAD)

Generally agreed upon by the tech community as the best bang for your buck, the GTX 1060 is a great addition to the build.

The mini version of this graphics card will fit snuggly inside the S4 Mini.

This beauty will cost you about $150 CAD.

Motherboard: MSI H270i Gaming Pro AC ($130 CAD)

The improved version of it's predecessor, the H170i, the H270i is the perfect mini-itx motherboard for this build.

Choosing this board will be important for properly orienting your components for an efficient build later on!

If you decided on the i7 7700K Processor for overclocking, swap out this board for the Z270i instead.

Same form factor, with overclock compatibility.

RAM: 16GB (2 x 8GB) ($80 CAD)

I forgot which brand of RAM I bought, but that doesn't matter!

Just make sure your RAM's height is lower than the case's.

Cooler: Noctua L9i ($50 CAD)

Like the case and power supply, you don't have much of a choice with this one.

Widely recommended by credible tech sources, this is the go-to fan for a slim and quiet experience.

Storage: M.2 250GB ($120 CAD)

The H270i Motherboard will allow for one M.2 storage stick. The 250GB is a great mid-ranged choice for most consumers.

Pro-tip: choose this storage as your boot-drive for a speedy PC turn-on.

SSD: 500GB ($80 CAD)

The S4 Mini will fit up to two SSDs (or an additional fan).

However, going for a budget build, we can shave off some dollars by going for a single SSD configuration.

PSU: Gury's G-Unique 300W ($150 CAD)

Guryhua from SFF Forums is running a one-man workshop to make some of the only custom Pico-style power supplies on the market, at an affordable price.

How much will it all cost?

Including the S4 Mini currently retailing at $199, a cheap $150 monitor and other peripherals valued at $200, the completed build will cost you about $1800CAD before tax.

Compared to the Aero 15X retailing at $2200USD before tax, that's a steal!

Not only do you get better functionality at a lower cost, the S4 Mini is still portable enough to fit into your bag, on the go.

At about the same dimensions as a Playstation, this rig is perfect for gamers and editors.

Bring this to your next LAN party and you're sure to get a few heads turning.
